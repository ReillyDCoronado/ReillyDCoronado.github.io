Creating a Model

Jumping into 3D modeling can be pretty intimidating, big, complicated programs, long online tutorials, boring online classes, whatever you may encounter when trying to start. It can be a lot to process, so here are some websites that you can try out right now, and learn how to use later.



TinkerCAD

TinkerCAD is a pretty easy thing to use. There is no vertex manipulation, you make models exclusively out shapes. Due to that, TinkerCAD is very easy to pick up and make whatever is on your mind, though of course because you cannot move individual vertices it's limited. But this does not mean you can't make interesting creations on it, some may just take more time and clever thinking.




Sketchup

SketchUp is not a site that I am familiar with, but I have heard and seen about it for a while, it seems to be made more towards creations such as architectural design. The site isn't limited to just that of course, it is however limited in many other ways unless you pay for a subscription, as many interesting features are locked behind paywalls. Despite this, after spending a few seconds in the program I was able to understand how to create objects, so with a little more time on the site I'm sure anyone could get the hang of it.




Vectary

Of these three recommendations Vectary is the one I'd suggest most, I'm most familiar with it, and for good reason. Of these sites, you can accomplish the most with Vectary, this is largely because of the ability to move individual vertices. You can start with a base shape(Admittedly, there are fewer shapes to choose from than TinkerCAD's huge database) and create anything you can imagine. Like Sketchup, Vectary has a free program for you to use and a subscription service. however unlike Sketchup, there are many more features that you get with the free version
