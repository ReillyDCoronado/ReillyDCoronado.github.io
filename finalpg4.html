3D Printing Machines

If you have a 3D model, whether you found it or you made it yourself, you may be interested in printing it out and getting to have it in real life. If that's the case, you may want to learn a thing or two about the two most popular forms or 3D printing.



Filament Printing

Filament 3D printing(FDM) takes a long spool of plastic, the machine melts the plastic and uses it to build up the 3D model's shape. Filament printing is used all over, filament printers are cheap, practical and reliable. With the right filament materials you can even make very strong models. However if you want to make small prints, or somes with fine details, you'll run into a problem, filament printers can only get so detailed before you lose a lot of the smaller parts of a model in the layers.



Resin Printing

Resin3D printing(SLA) uses liquid UV resin, while printing the a UV light causes the resin to turn into a solid polymer. One of the biggst pros of resin printing is resolution, there is a level of detail in resin printing that is lost with filaments. However, resin printers generally can only build smaller projects due to smaller buildlates that filament printers. They are also often slower, and 3D prints require washing and further curing after it is completed to get a proper print.


Which kind of printer you want is always up to you, but its good to know your options well!
